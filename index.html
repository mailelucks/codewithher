Our Philosophy

Together with our Code girl community, we are on the the pursuit of the following principles:

Possibility

Expand the perspective of girls and women to the endless opportunities in the technology field.

Leadership

Develop creativity, courage and confidence in all participants to be the tech leaders of tomorrow.

Impact

Increase the amount of young women interested in pursuing a career in technology.

Inclusiveness

Promote equity, diversity and equality in technology education and opportunities.

Community

Create a supportive environment and strong networks of women in technology. where we are empowered to speak our minds, celebrate our successes as a team, and to support each other through our journey.

Code With Her provides real-world coding experiences to empower your child to be the next leader in technology

Girl Scout Badge Workshops

All of our programs are in line with the Girl Scout Leadership Experience and taught by women mentors in the technology field. Girl Scouts will learn how to use technology as a tool to make the world a better place

Take Action with Technology!

Juniors, Cadettes, Seniors & Ambassadors
Duration: 2hrs

Learn how to use technology as a tool to make the world a better place! Explore apps, identify an issue in your community, and prototype an app as a solution to that issue. A great intro for technology!

Think like a Programmer

All
Duration: 2.5hrs

Start your computer science journey with women in the field! Girls will connect with computer science, & uncover the skills it takes to think like a programmer! Take Action project not included.

Cybersecurity

Daisy, Brownie, & Juniors
Duration: 1.5hrs

NEW! Learn online safety and privacy principles, how the internet works, and spotting & investigating cybercrime. Each age level has a 3 badge series: Basics, Safeguards, & Investigators

Netiquette

Cadette
Duration: 1.5hrs

Learn how to make positive choices in the online world by earning this badge. Girls will learn to stay in touch and share ideas safely through social media, emailing and blogging.

Technovation Challenge & Highest Awards App Creation

Cadettes, Seniors & Ambassadors
Duration: TBD

Change the world through technology by designing and coding your own app through MIT App Inventor. To create a sustainable change, inform people using your coding skills. Contact us for pricing and timeline

Coding Together

All & Adults
Duration: 2hrs

We believe that it takes a village to empower our future women tech leaders! You and your Girl Scout will be able to participate in a hands-on workshop, coding together, using Scratch.

$20/pair for 8+ pairs, $25/pair minimum 5 pairs*

Our Team of Technology Professionals

All of our programs are in line with the Girl Scout leadership experience and are taught by female mentors in the technology field.

Kylie Taitano

Co-Founder and Instructor

Kylie has nearly 10 years of experience organizing and advocating for the advancement of women and education in tech. She discovered her passion for these issues in her first year as a Computer Science major at UCSD. Kylie was selected as the Secretary for the founding board of UCSD’s undergraduate Women in Computing group, serving as President in her senior year. During her time with WIC, she worked with her board to create several programs and events to recruit, retain, and support women in tech, including raising scholarship funds to send over a dozen students to the 2013 Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing.

Kylie currently works as a Software Engineer at Intuit; outside of her daily work, she spent three years as a member of the Intuit Tech Women of Intuit core team focusing on K-12 outreach. In this role, she partnered with Girl Scouts San Diego to help Girl Scout troops across San Diego discover the limitless opportunities in tech through various programs like the You Can Be in Tech day conference and the 12-week Technovation Challenge program. Kylie also participates as a current board member of the UCSD Computer Science and Education Alumni Advisory Board. She has been recognized for her advocacy work, helping Intuit to be recognized as one of 2017 Community Partner of the Year, and receiving Athena San Diego’s inaugural Pinnacle Award for Rising Star in 2019.

Manisha Aswani

Co-Founder and Instructor

Manisha is a Director for Technical Program Management at Teradata. Manisha is very passionate about teaching. She believes that Code With Her provides an ecosystem where kids can learn to code via integrated learning and how leverage the skills in their daily lives. Due to her background in software engineering, she understands the complexities of programming. She believes Code With Her addresses the complexities of engineering and makes programming easier for kids to understand while also having fun.

Manisha is a mom to two kids who are in elementary school. She is involved in her kids' school activities in addition to soccer, swimming and gymnastics.

Michaela Mitchell

Co-Founder and Instructor

Michaela is an Account Marketing Manager at the San Diego Blood Bank. After graduating with a bachelor's degree in Business Administration from Washington State University , she served as a Community Economic Development volunteer in Peace Corps Costa Rica. After, she worked for Girl Scouts San Diego where she learned the true meaning of STEMinism, and has since been an advocate for girls of all backgrounds to have opportunities in the technology field. Michaela has served as the co-chair for the Women in STEM committee in the San Diego STEM Ecosystem and was a Mentor for the Technovation Challenge.

Maile Lucks

Instructor

Maile is a frontend web developer at Miva, a San Diego based ecommerce software company, where she builds out online stores using the Miva Merchant platform in conjunction with several coding languages. Maile graduated from Towson State University with a degree in Television Production. She later decided to become a web developer and taught herself how to code. Maile is a strong advocate for self-learning and empowering students to take initiative and acquire new skills using nothing more than Google and a curious mind. When she isn't coding, Maile can be found hiking or trying new food.
